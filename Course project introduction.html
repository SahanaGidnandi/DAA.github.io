Suryanagari - E-Commerce Logistics and Customer Experience

Course Project Introduction

Problem Space and How It Was Arrived At

The problem space revolves around enhancing e-commerce logistics and customer experience in the context of a smart city. The problem was identified based on the growing demand for efficient logistics and personalized customer service in a rapidly developing urban environment. As e-commerce platforms become increasingly complex, the logistics of handling vast amounts of products, customer demands, and delivery routes must be optimized.

Findings from the White Paper

The white paper summarized the key challenges in e-commerce logistics, especially in urban settings. Major findings included the need for real-time delivery optimization, dynamic pricing based on demand, fraud detection mechanisms, and efficient product management systems. Algorithms such as Dijkstra’s, Bellman-Ford, and dynamic programming techniques were identified as crucial in optimizing logistics and operations.

Reflection on City Design and Business Case Identification

In the context of city design, we considered factors like urban transportation systems, data collection via sensors, customer demand fluctuations, and the availability of real-time data to improve the e-commerce experience. Business cases were identified by analyzing operational bottlenecks, customer needs, and technological feasibility. Each case focuses on an area where algorithmic solutions can make the biggest impact.

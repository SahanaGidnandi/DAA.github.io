Fraud Detection: Purpose & Usage of Algorithms

Purpose:

Fraud detection identifies unusual or suspicious patterns in transactions, helping to prevent fraud and financial losses. By analyzing transaction data with algorithms, the system can flag suspicious activities for further review. The goal is to detect fraudulent behavior early, reducing risks for businesses and customers.

Usage of Portable Tools:

Prim’s Algorithm:

Prim’s algorithm is used to find the minimum spanning tree (MST) of a weighted graph. In fraud detection, it can be used to model relationships between various entities (such as customers or accounts) based on transaction patterns. The algorithm helps identify the most connected entities that may have higher risks of fraudulent activities.

Kruskal’s Algorithm:

Kruskal's algorithm helps in detecting the smallest "cliques" or clusters of nodes (transactions or accounts) that are potentially suspicious. It detects fraud patterns by linking accounts or transactions that are highly interconnected, helping to detect hidden fraud rings.

Floyd-Warshall Algorithm:

Floyd-Warshall is an all-pairs shortest path algorithm that calculates the shortest paths between every pair of nodes in a graph. It helps detect indirect relationships that may indicate fraudulent activities by examining the connections between transactions or accounts.
